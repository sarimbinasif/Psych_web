Providing solutions for healthier tech habits

Stages of tech addictivity



Under use

Limited Productivity: Reluctance or inability to leverage technology for efficiency. Outdated Knowledge: Falling behind in acquiring and using current digital tools. Communication Gaps: Missing out on opportunities for online collaboration and communication. Manual Repetition: Performing tasks manually that could be automated with technology.



Balanced

Efficient Workflow: Using technology to streamline tasks and enhance productivity. Mindful Consumption: Being aware of and managing screen time for a healthy balance. Effective Communication: Leveraging technology for clear and efficient communication. Adaptive Learning: Embracing new tech tools for ongoing personal and professional development.



Over use

Excessive Screen Time: Spending a disproportionate amount of time on digital devices. Social Withdrawal: Preferring online interactions over face-to-face communication. Sleep Disturbances: Disrupted sleep patterns due to late-night technology use. Reduced Productivity: Technology use interfering with work or daily responsibilities.

How to overcome?

  1. Regularly exercise

    2. Incorporate physical activities into your routine. Exercise not only promotes
    physical health but also provides a break from screens.

  2. Unplug before bed

    3. Create a technology-free routine before bedtime to improve sleep quality.
    Avoid screens at least an hour before going to sleep.

  3. Socialize offline

    4. Make an effort to spend quality time with friends and family in person,
    fostering meaningful connections beyond digital communication.

  4. Set timer

    5. Establish specific limits for daily or weekly screen time. Use built-in
    device features or apps to track and manage your usage.

  5. Go for alternatives

    6. Diversify activities beyond tech for a mindful, fulfilling life.
    Engage outdoors, read, and prioritize face-to-face interactions to reconnect with reality.
