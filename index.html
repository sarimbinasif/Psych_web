Providing solutions for healthier tech habits

Stages of tech addictivity



Under use

Productivity: Means you are not using technology for efficiency.
Communication Gaps: Increases communication gap between you and your colleagues/classmates.

Manual Repetition: You have to perform tasks manually that could be automated with technology.



Balanced

Efficient Workflow: Using technology to streamline tasks and enhance productivity.
Mindful Consumption: Means a person is aware of managing screen time.

Adaptive Learning: Allows you to learn new tech tools for personal and professional development.



Over use

Social Withdrawal: You start preferring online interactions over face-to-face communication.
Sleep Disturbances: Disrupted sleep patterns due to late-night technology use.
Reduced Productivity: Technology use interfering with work or daily responsibilities.

How to overcome?

  1. Regularly exercise

    2. Incorporate physical activities into your routine. Exercise not only promotes
    physical health but also provides a break from screens.

  2. Unplug before bed

    3. Create a technology-free routine before bedtime to improve sleep quality.
    Avoid screens at least an hour before going to sleep.

  3. Socialize offline

    4. Make an effort to spend quality time with friends and family in person,
    fostering meaningful connections beyond digital communication.

  4. Set timer

    5. Establish specific limits for daily or weekly screen time. Use built-in
    device features or apps to track and manage your usage.

  5. Go for alternatives

    6. Diversify activities beyond tech for a mindful, fulfilling life.
    Engage outdoors, read, and prioritize face-to-face interactions to reconnect with reality.
